About the Fort Collins R Collab

The Fort Collins R Collaborative is part of an R Consortium-funded grant that supports community-led, locally-organized R development events around the world, otherwise known as R Community Collaboratives.

At the Fort Collins R Collab, you'll team up with R users and developers of all skill levels. Together, you will create a valuable data product that will contribute directly to a research team at Colorado State University.

This year's theme will be Sports and Movement, and participants at the event will work on developing R code and methods to explore and analyze data on this topic contributed by researchers from Colorado State University. Whether you are an experienced developer or an R-user-to-be, you have something to contribute to this event. Our event is open to a wide range of experience levels and attendee backgrounds, and we seek to encourage diverse groups to contribute to the larger R community.

If you’re interested in learning more about what you’ll be doing at the event, take a look at the schedule for other R Collaborative events, including last year's Chicago R Collaborative, peruse our Code of Conduct, or reach out to corcollab [at] gmail.com if you have questions!

Join us!

Applications for the For Collins R Collab are open! The application form should take around 15–20 minutes to fill out and will help us gauge your interest and involvement in the R community. The deadline for applications is midnight MST on March 6, 2020 and we plan to notify participants by March 13, 2020.
